## Perioperative Data Science & Consciousness Science

O'Reilly-Shah Lab is directed by [Dr. Vikas N. O'Reilly-Shah](https://faculty.washington.edu/vors/), Professor of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine at the University of Washington. The lab operates at the intersection of two programs: perioperative data science and consciousness science.

**Perioperative data science.** We develop and evaluate machine learning, natural language processing, and large-scale analytic methods to improve surgical and anesthetic outcomes. This work spans ASA Physical Status prediction from clinical notes, cardiac arrest prediction from EHR data, LLM-based clinical risk stratification, and national-scale quality benchmarking through the Multicenter Perioperative Outcomes Group (MPOG).

**Consciousness science.** We develop the State Space Theory (SST) of consciousness, a theory proposing that consciousness is constituted by hierarchical delay coordinate embedding in plastic recurrent neural networks. SST is grounded in a novel metaphysical framework, Computational Dynamic Monism (CDM). We pursue the mathematical foundations, philosophical implications, and computational experiments that follow from this theory.

We also build clinical software used worldwide, including the Anesthesiologist app (500,000+ downloads) and open-source tools for perioperative research.
