I am a pediatric anesthesiologist at Seattle Children's Hospital and Professor of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine at the University of Washington. I take care of children in the OR and I study the physical basis of consciousness.

The through-line across my work is dynamical systems. The same mathematics that describes how brains reconstruct the world from sensory streams also governs perioperative physiology, the hidden states of language models, and the recurrent dynamics of anesthetic depth. Delay coordinate embedding, attractor geometry, topological invariants: these are the shared structure.

My two main research programs are **consciousness science** and **perioperative data science**. On the consciousness side, I developed the State Space Theory (SST), published in the *Journal of Consciousness Studies*, which proposes that consciousness is constituted by hierarchical delay coordinate embedding in plastic recurrent neural networks. On the perioperative side, I work on machine learning, NLP, and multicenter analytics for surgical and anesthetic outcomes, with publications in *JAMA Surgery*, *Circulation*, and others.

I run [PPiQSO](https://ppiqso.org) (Center for Pain and Perioperative initiatives in Quality Safety and Outcomes) at UW, chair the ASA Committee on Informatics, and built the [Anesthesiologist app](/software/), which has been downloaded over 500,000 times.

I did my MD/PhD at Vanderbilt through the MSTP and residency at Stanford. I am a Santa Fe Institute alumnus. I hold board certifications in anesthesiology, pediatric anesthesiology, and clinical informatics. Outside the main research programs, I have published on the use of emoji in biomedical literature and co-authored a book of bee puns.
