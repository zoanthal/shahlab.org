## Collaborators

### Alessandro Selvitella, Ph.D.

Department of Mathematical Sciences, Purdue University Fort Wayne. Collaboration on the mathematical foundations of the State Space Theory of consciousness, including smooth embeddings in contracting recurrent networks. Joint work on philosophy of mind: the unfolding argument against computational theories of consciousness, and a reexamination of Searle's Chinese Room argument through the lens of dynamical systems theory.

### Aaron Schurger, Ph.D.

Cognitive Neuroimaging Unit, INSERM-CEA, Paris. Collaboration on the unfolding argument and the role of plasticity in computational theories of consciousness. Joint work examines how recurrent networks with plasticity generate state space structures that feedforward unfoldings cannot reproduce, with implications for theories that identify consciousness with computational organization.

### Stanley Krippner, Ph.D.

Saybrook University. Collaboration on the Chaos-State Space Dream Model, integrating phenomenological accounts of dream experience with neurodynamic models of sleep-state transitions. This model connects SST's state space framework to the phenomenology of dreaming, lucid dreaming, and altered states of consciousness.

### Philip Chung, M.D.

Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine, University of Washington. Bonica Scholar. Collaboration on NLP for perioperative risk stratification and evaluation of LLM capabilities in clinical prediction tasks, including ASA Physical Status assignment and perioperative outcome prediction.

### Craig Jabaley, M.D.

Department of Anesthesiology, Emory University. Long-standing collaboration spanning perioperative informatics, sepsis research, mobile health applications, and sugammadex studies in both adult and pediatric populations.

### Deepak Long, M.D.

Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine, University of Washington. Collaboration on COVID-19 perioperative research and NLP-based surgical site infection detection from clinical notes.

### Andrew Walters, M.D.

Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine, University of Washington. Associate Medical Director of PPiQSO (Perioperative and Pain Improvement, Quality, Safety, and Outcomes). Collaboration on perioperative quality measurement and outcomes research.

### Monica Vavilala, M.D.

Department of Anesthesiology and Pain Medicine, University of Washington. Collaboration on neurocritical care research.
