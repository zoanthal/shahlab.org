## Pediatric Anesthesiology & Perioperative Quality

Dr. O'Reilly-Shah is a practicing pediatric anesthesiologist and Chair of the MPOG ASPIRE Pediatric Quality Committee. This research program focuses on improving perioperative outcomes for children through evidence-based quality metrics, clinical studies, and national benchmarking.

### MPOG ASPIRE Pediatric Quality

The Multicenter Perioperative Outcomes Group (MPOG) maintains the Anesthesiology Performance Improvement and Reporting Exchange (ASPIRE), a national quality benchmarking program. As Chair of the Pediatric Quality Committee, Dr. O'Reilly-Shah leads the development and validation of pediatric-specific quality measures across dozens of academic medical centers. This work defines standards for pediatric anesthesia care and identifies opportunities for improvement at the national level.

### Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS)

We study enhanced recovery protocols in pediatric surgical populations, evaluating bundled interventions designed to reduce length of stay, opioid consumption, and postoperative complications.

### Sugammadex and Neuromuscular Blockade

Our group has published on sugammadex use in pediatric populations, examining dosing, efficacy, safety, and cost-effectiveness compared to traditional reversal agents. This work includes both single-center studies and multi-institutional analyses.

### Perioperative Hypothermia

Inadvertent perioperative hypothermia is a common and preventable complication in pediatric patients. We study its incidence, risk factors, and the effectiveness of warming interventions using large perioperative datasets.

### Quality Improvement Initiatives

Beyond measurement, we implement and study quality improvement interventions in pediatric perioperative care, including standardized handoff protocols, medication safety initiatives, and workflow optimization.
