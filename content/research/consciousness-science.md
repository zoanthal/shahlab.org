## Consciousness Science

### State Space Theory (SST)

The State Space Theory of consciousness proposes that consciousness is constituted by hierarchical delay coordinate embedding in plastic recurrent neural networks. Delay coordinate embedding is a technique from dynamical systems theory: given a time series of observations, one reconstructs the underlying attractor geometry by arranging time-delayed copies of the signal into a higher-dimensional space. SST holds that biological neural networks perform this operation on their own activity, and that the resulting reconstructed state space is the physical substrate of conscious experience.

SST was published in the *Journal of Consciousness Studies* in 2025. A unification paper extending the theory and connecting it to existing frameworks in consciousness science is forthcoming in *Nonlinear Dynamics, Psychology, and Life Sciences* (2026).

### Computational Dynamic Monism (CDM)

Computational Dynamic Monism is the metaphysical framework underlying SST. CDM holds that computational and physical descriptions of neural processes are two aspects of a single substrate, unified through the mathematics of dynamical systems. CDM avoids both substance dualism and eliminativism by grounding mental properties in the topological and geometric structure of neural state spaces. A paper developing CDM in detail has been submitted to *Synthese* (2025).

### Mathematical Foundations

With Alessandro Selvitella (Purdue University Fort Wayne), we study the mathematical conditions under which smooth embeddings exist in contracting recurrent networks. This work provides the formal underpinnings for SST by characterizing the class of dynamical systems capable of supporting delay coordinate reconstructions under biologically plausible constraints. A paper on smooth embeddings has been submitted to *PLOS Computational Biology* (2026).

### The Unfolding Argument

With Alessandro Selvitella and Aaron Schurger (INSERM-CEA, Paris), we examine the unfolding argument against computational theories of consciousness. The unfolding argument, originally due to Doerig et al. (2019), claims that any feedforward network can replicate the input-output mapping of a recurrent network, and therefore computational structure is insufficient to distinguish conscious from non-conscious systems. Our work demonstrates that this argument fails when plasticity and temporal dynamics are taken into account: recurrent networks with plasticity generate state space structures that feedforward unfoldings cannot reproduce.

### Chinese Rooms

With Alessandro Selvitella, we revisit Searle's Chinese Room argument in light of SST and dynamical systems theory. The paper argues that Searle's thought experiment conflates static symbol manipulation with the temporally extended, geometrically structured computation that SST identifies as constitutive of understanding. The Chinese Room, as described, lacks the recurrent dynamics and plasticity required to generate the relevant state space embeddings.
