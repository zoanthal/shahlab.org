## Perioperative Data Science & Clinical Informatics

We develop and evaluate machine learning, natural language processing, and large-scale analytic approaches to improve perioperative care. Our work draws on electronic health record data from single-center studies to national multicenter registries.

### ASA Physical Status and NLP

The American Society of Anesthesiologists Physical Status (ASA-PS) classification is the most widely used preoperative risk metric. We developed NLP and machine learning pipelines to predict ASA-PS directly from clinical text, demonstrating that automated systems can approximate physician-assigned scores and exposing systematic variation in how clinicians assign these scores. More recent work evaluates the performance of large language models (GPT-4, Claude, open-source models) on perioperative risk prediction tasks, comparing LLM outputs against validated scoring systems and physician judgment.

### Cardiac Arrest Prediction

We built machine learning models for intraoperative cardiac arrest prediction using EHR data, identifying temporal risk patterns and high-risk phenotypes that precede arrest events.

### Perioperative Quality and MPOG/ASPIRE

Dr. O'Reilly-Shah serves as Chair of the MPOG ASPIRE Pediatric Quality Committee. Through this role, the lab contributes to national perioperative quality benchmarking across dozens of academic medical centers, developing and validating quality measures for pediatric anesthesia care.

### Bias and Ethics in Automated Risk Assessment

Automated risk scores can encode and amplify biases present in training data. We study how race, ethnicity, insurance status, and socioeconomic factors interact with algorithmic risk prediction in perioperative settings, and we develop frameworks for auditing these systems.
